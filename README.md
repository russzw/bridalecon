## ğŸŒ What Is Bride Price?

Bride price is a traditional practice in many societies where the groom or his family provides payment (in cash, livestock, or goods) to the brideâ€™s family as part of formalizing a marriage. It is distinct from dowry and is deeply embedded in social structures, kinship systems, and gender economics.

BridalEcon aims to:
- Document regional variations in bride price customs
- Analyze economic implications (e.g., inflation, commercialization, gender equity)
- Visualize data through an interactive **3D globe**
- Provide historical and anthropological context
